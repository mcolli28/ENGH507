Commute. Coffee. Convenience.

The morning commute, while not always fun, can be a period of consistency in an otherwise ever-changing work environment. When you have no ideas what each day might bring, you can generally count on your walk to the metro stop, the words “this is a 7000 series train,” as you board your car, and generally, a consistent timeline until you reach your office’s station. Although public transportation on the Metro can often be less than glamorous, it does afford most of us a period of isolation to compose ourselves before the start of the day. For some people, this could mean getting some work done before arriving at the office and being subjected to morning chit chat. For others it means finishing your favorite podcast or listening to a playlist to get pumped up for the day. But for many, it means enjoying some coffee while having some time to yourself in the morning. Whether you walk by a metro stop on your way to work or ride the metro daily, many Metro stops are centered in urban areas with many coffee shops available. On this website, you’ll be able to look at a curated list of local coffee shops located along each of the underground stations on the Metro Orange Line from the following stops:

  • Ballston
  • Virginia Sqaure
  • Clarendon
  • Courthouse
  • Rosslyn

Ballston

The first underground stop on the orange/silver line in what is know as the Ballston-Rosslyn corridor.

Virginia Square

A somewhat sleepier stop than the rest on the line.

Clarendon

Typicall known for its night life, this stop has plenty to offer commuters as well.

Courthouse

Named after the Arlington Courthouse, this is a stop frequently used by commuters.

Rosslyn

The final stop in Virginia, the surronding area is a huge business and commuter hub.
