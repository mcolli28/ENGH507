Education History

K–12

I underwent a traditional schooling experience in my hometown of Salem, VA, where I began kindergarten and graduated high school in the same public school system. At Salem High School I took a mix of standard and advanced classes, called International Baccalaureate, or IB classes. many of the IB classes were dual enrolled with the local community college. Because of this, I transferred many credits to my Christopher Newport University, where I had decided to go to undergrad.

Undergrad

Since I started college with so many credits, I was able to skip many of my general education requirements and begin working on my degrees early. Thanks to my academic advistor, I realized I could easily pursue two degrees within four years. I decided to pursue both history and communication degrees. CNU is a public University located in Newport News, VA and is known for the following attributes:

  • The Great Lawn - A large patch of grass that functions as the center of the campus. Rumored to have spray-painted grass to make it appear greener.
  • The Commons - A dining hall known for fast-food style meals like burgers and fries
  • Columns - The neo-classical architecture used at CNU means you'll always have a column in your line of site.
CNU Fact

My final semester at CNU was also my most eventful one. During this semester, I completed my communication thesis, was selected to present my thesis at my school's academic conference, made Dean's list, and graduated!

The Road to Graduate School

In 2014, I graduated with both degrees and joined the work force. I had always planned on attending graduate school, but I was eager to use my skills in a professional setting and wanted to wait for better timing to join a program.

Eventually, I was able to apply to George Mason University's English program, which aligns with my professional life, and will teach me many skills to be a better worker.
